EPIDEMIOLOGY

Epidemiology is the study of incidence and distribution of disease.
Either clinical or community-based populations are studied.
Our patients are part of a clinical population.
However, most epidemiologic studies use community populations.
Clinical populations often have higher rates of disorders than community populations.

National Comorbidity Survey (NCS)

A US household survey with 8,098 respondents, ages 15-54.
It was found that alcohol-use disorders are highly correlated with other Axis I diagnoses.
Also, having a mood, anxiety or thought disorder strongly predicts future development of an alcohol-use disorder, especially in women.
Mood, anxiety and thought disorders increase risk of developing an alcohol-use disorder, especially in women.
Anxiety disorders are more common in women while alcohol-use disorders are more common in men.
Men with alcohol dependence have 2x the odds of an anxiety disorder, vs. men without.
Women with alcohol dependence have 3x the odds of having an anxiety disorder, vs. women without.
Having an anxiety disorder more strongly predicts development of an alcohol-use disorder in women (vs. in men).
